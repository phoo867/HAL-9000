Portugese Egg Tart

Indulge in a taste of Portugal with the irresistible charm of Portuguese Egg Tarts. These delightful pastries, also known as Pastéis de Nata, offer a heavenly combination of flaky pastry and luscious custard filling. Get ready to experience a burst of sweet and creamy flavors that will transport you straight to the sunny streets of Lisbon.

Lisbon Love: Portuguese Egg Tarts to Sweeten Your Day!

Preparation Time: 30 minutes
Cooking Time: 20 minutes

Get ready to embark on a mouthwatering journey to Portugal with these delectable Portuguese Egg Tarts. These sweet and creamy custard-filled pastries are a beloved treat that will transport you to the charming streets of Lisbon

Join me in the kitchen as we whip up a batch of these delightful tarts that are sure to bring joy to your taste buds!

Instruction

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Begin by making the pie crust. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

Gradually add the cold water, a tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Shape the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 15 minutes.

While the dough chills, prepare the filling. In a saucepan, whisk together the granulated sugar and cornstarch. Gradually add the milk, whisking continuously to avoid any lumps.

Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook the mixture, stirring constantly, until it thickens and comes to a gentle boil. Remove from heat.

In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks. Gradually add a spoonful of the hot milk mixture to the egg yolks while whisking vigorously. This step helps temper the eggs and prevent scrambling.

Gradually pour the tempered egg mixture back into the saucepan while whisking constantly. Place the saucepan back on low heat and cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously, until the custard thickens.

Remove the custard from heat and stir in the vanilla extract and lemon zest. Let it cool for a few minutes.

Take the chilled dough from the refrigerator and roll it out on a floured surface until it is about 1/8 inch thick. Cut out rounds slightly larger than the cavities of a muffin tin.

Gently press the dough rounds into the muffin tin, shaping them into small tart shells.

Pour the cooled custard into the prepared tart shells, filling them about 3/4 of the way.

Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the custard is set and the pastry is golden brown.

Remove the tarts from the oven and let them cool in the muffin tin for a few minutes. Then, transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Just before serving, dust the tarts with a sprinkle of cinnamon powder. Now, savor the Lisbon love with these delectable Portuguese Egg Tarts!

Ingredients


For the dough:

  • All-purpose flour 2 cups
  • Ice water 1/4 cup
  • Unsalted butter ½ cup, cold and cubed
  • Salt ½ teaspoon

For the filling:

  • Granulated sugar 1 cup
  • Cornstarch 1/4 cup
  • Large egg yolks 4
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
  • Milk 2 cups
