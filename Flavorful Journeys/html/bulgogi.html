Bulgogi

Bulgogi is a traditional Korean dish that consists of marinated grilled or stir-fried beef. The name "bulgogi" literally means "fire meat" in Korean, and the dish is known for its sweet and savory flavor profile. The marinade is typically made with soy sauce, sugar, garlic, sesame oil, and other spices, and the beef is often served with rice, lettuce leaves, and other side dishes. Bulgogi is a popular dish in Korea and has gained popularity around the world due to its delicious taste and simple yet flavorful preparation.

Sweet and Savory Bulgogi Stir-Fry


Preparation time: 10 minutes

Cooking time: 15 minutes


Craving something sweet, savory, and oh-so-satisfying? This Bulgogi stir-fry is just what you need!

Made with marinated beef and a flavorful assortment of veggies, this dish is sure to please your taste buds and leave you feeling full and satisfied. Best of all, it's quick and easy to make, so you can enjoy this delicious meal any day of the week.

Instruction

Start by making the marinade. In a mixing bowl, combine the soy sauce, brown sugar, honey, sesame oil, minced garlic, grated ginger, and red pepper flakes. Mix well.

Add the sliced beef to the marinade and toss to coat. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight in the refrigerator.

In a large pan or wok, heat up the vegetable oil over high heat. Add the sliced bell peppers and onion, and stir-fry for 5-7 minutes, until they are tender-crisp.

Remove the veggies from the pan and set them aside.

Add the marinated beef to the pan and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until it is browned and cooked through.

Add the cooked veggies back to the pan, and stir-fry for an additional minute or so until everything is well combined.

Serve the bulgogi stir-fry over a bed of cooked rice, and enjoy your delicious and savory meal!

Ingredients

  • Beef sirloin, sliced thinly 1 pound
  • Soy sauce 1/4 cup
  • Brown sugar 2 tablespoons
  • Honey 1 tablespoon
  • Sesame oil 1 tablespoon
  • Garlic cloves, minced 2
  • Grated ginger 1 tablespoon
  • Garlic cloves, minced 2
  • Red pepper flakes 1/2 teaspoon
  • Vegetable oil 2 tablespoons
  • Red bell pepper, sliced 1
  • Green bell pepper, sliced 1
  • Onion, sliced 1
  • Cooked rice 2 cups
