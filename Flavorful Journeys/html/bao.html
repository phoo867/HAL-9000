Bao

Bao, also known as steamed buns or baozi, is a type of Chinese bread made with fluffy, white dough that is steamed instead of baked. Bao can be filled with a variety of sweet or savory fillings, such as pork, beef, chicken, vegetables, or sweet bean paste. They are a popular street food in China and have gained popularity in other parts of the world as well. Bao is often enjoyed as a snack or as part of a meal, and is known for its soft, pillowy texture and delicious fillings.

Fluffy Bao Buns with BBQ Pork Filling


Preparation time: 30 minutes

Cooking time: 20 minutes


Get ready to steam up some delicious bao buns filled with savory BBQ pork!

These fluffy, pillowy buns are a popular street food in China and can be filled with a variety of sweet or savory fillings. Perfect for a fun and flavorful snack or as part of a meal, these bao buns are sure to satisfy your cravings.

Instruction

Start by making the bao dough. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, instant yeast, and salt. Add in the warm water and vegetable oil, and mix until the dough comes together.

Knead the dough for about 5-10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic. Cover the dough with a damp towel and let it rest for 10 minutes.

While the dough is resting, let's prepare the BBQ pork filling. In a pan, heat up the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the cooked and shredded BBQ pork, soy sauce, hoisin sauce, and brown sugar. Stir and cook for 5 minutes until the mixture is heated through and the flavors are combined. Remove from heat and let it cool.

Divide the bao dough into 12 equal pieces, and shape each piece into a ball. Roll each ball out into a flat circle, about 4 inches in diameter.

Place a tablespoon of the BBQ pork filling in the center of each dough circle. Pinch the edges of the dough together to seal the filling inside and form a ball shape.

Place the bao buns on a sheet of parchment paper, and let them rest for 10 minutes.

In a steamer basket, steam the bao buns for 10-12 minutes, until they are fluffy and cooked through.

Serve the bao buns hot, and enjoy your delicious and savory BBQ pork-filled bao buns!

Ingredients


For the Bao Buns:

  • All-purpose flour 2 cups
  • Sugar 2 tablespoons
  • Instant yeast 1 teaspoon
  • Warm water 1/2 cup
  • Vegetable oil 1 tablespoon
  • Salt Pinch

For the BBQ Pork Filling:

  • Cooked and shredded BBQ pork 1 cup
  • Green onions, sliced 2
  • Soy sauce 1 tablespoon
  • Hoisin sauce 1 tablespoon
  • Brown sugar 1 tablespoon
  • Vegetable oil 1 tablespoon
