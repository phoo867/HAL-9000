Biryani

Biryani is a popular South Asian dish particularly in India and Pakistan. It consists of layers of fragrant rice cooked with aromatic spices, tender meat or vegetables, and sometimes nuts and dried fruit. It's known for its delicious taste and has become a staple in South Asian cuisine.

Spicy and Aromatic Biryani


Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 40 minutes


Biryani is a classic South Asian dish that's loved for its fragrant rice, aromatic spices, and tender meat or vegetables.

This recipe puts a fun twist on the classic dish, with a blend of spices and herbs that add a touch of heat and flavor. It's perfect for a special occasion or for serving up something different for dinner.

Instruction

Start by rinsing the basmati rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Cook the rice according to the package instructions, using 1 1/2 cups of water for every 1 cup of rice. Once cooked, fluff the rice with a fork and set it aside.

In a large pan, heat up the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for 3-4 minutes, until it becomes soft and translucent.

Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, cumin, coriander, garam masala, turmeric, and cayenne pepper to the pan. Stir everything together and cook for another 2-3 minutes, until the spices are fragrant.

Add the chicken or lamb pieces to the pan and stir everything together to coat the meat in the spices. Cook for about 10 minutes, until the meat is browned on all sides.

Reduce the heat to low and add the yogurt to the pan. Stir everything together and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes, until the meat is cooked through.

Layer the cooked rice and meat mixture in a large serving dish, starting with a layer of rice, followed by a layer of meat, and repeating until everything is used up.

Cover the dish with foil and place it in the oven. Bake for 20-25 minutes at 350 degrees Fahrenheit, until everything is heated through and the flavors have melded together.

Garnish the biryani with fresh cilantro and serve hot.

Ingredients


  • Basmati rice 2 cups
  • Vegetable oil 2 tablespoons
  • Onion, chopped 1
  • Garlic cloves, minced 2
  • Grated ginger 1 tablespoon
  • Ground cumin 1 teaspoon
  • Ground coriander 1 teaspoon
  • Garam masala 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric 1/2 tablespoon
  • Cayenne pepper 1/2 teaspoon
  • Chicken or lamb, 1 pound
  • Plain yogurt 1/2 cup
  • Honey 1/4 cup
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped 1/2 cup
