Samosas

Samosas are a popular snack and appetizer that originated in South Asia, particularly in India and Pakistan. They consist of a crispy pastry shell filled with a savory mixture of spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat or other vegetables. Samosas are usually served with a side of chutney or yogurt dip, and are loved for their delicious flavor and crispy texture. They have become a popular snack around the world and are often enjoyed as a quick and tasty snack.

Crunchy and Delicious Samosas for a Tasty Snack


Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 20 minutes


Samosas are a classic South Asian snack that are loved for their crispy pastry shell and savory filling.

This recipe puts a fun twist on the classic dish, with a filling that's loaded with flavorful spices and vegetables. These samosas are perfect for a quick snack or for serving as a party appetizer.

Instruction

Start by boiling the diced potatoes in a pot of salted water until they are soft. Drain the potatoes and set them aside.

In a large pan, heat up some vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for 3-4 minutes, until it becomes soft and translucent.

Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, cumin, coriander, turmeric, and cayenne pepper to the pan. Stir everything together and cook for another 2-3 minutes, until the spices are fragrant.

Add the cooked potatoes and peas to the pan and stir everything together to coat the vegetables in the spices. Cook for 2-3 minutes, until the vegetables are well coated and heated through. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Roll out the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface and cut it into squares. Spoon a tablespoon of the vegetable mixture into the center of each pastry square.

Fold the pastry over to form a triangle shape and press the edges together to seal.

Heat up some vegetable oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the samosas to the pan and fry them for 2-3 minutes on each side, until they are golden brown and crispy.

Remove the samosas from the pan with a slotted spoon and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain off any excess oil.

Serve the samosas hot with a side of chutney or yogurt dip.

Ingredients


  • frozen puff pastry, thawed 1 package
  • potatoes, peeled and diced 2
  • Onion, chopped 1
  • Garlic cloves, minced 2
  • peas 1/2 cup
  • Grated ginger 1 tablespoon
  • Ground cumin 1 teaspoon
  • Ground coriander/strong> 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric 1/2 tablespoon
  • Cayenne pepper 1/4 teaspoon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying
